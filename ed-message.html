Four Decades of Dedicated Service to Sri Lanka’s Elderly

A Message from the Executive Director – Dr. Harsha Bandara

It is with great pleasure that I pen this message to the HelpAge Website for information of the viewers.

The first World Assembly on Ageing held in Vienna in 1982 involving member states of the United Nations focused on addressing ageing-related challenges and led to 62 recommendations for research, education, health, welfare, and income security of the elderly.

Following these principles, HelpAge International (UK) was founded in 1983, and HelpAge Sri Lanka was established in 1986 to ensure older persons lead dignified, active, and healthy lives. In 2010, HelpAge Sri Lanka became an affiliate of HelpAge International, England, and has since served the nation’s elders for nearly four decades.

Key Services and Programmes

  • Eye Hospital: Located in Wellawatte, performs over 400 free cataract surgeries per month, totaling 50,000 by May 2024.
  • Mobile Medical Unit (MMU): Provides free medical and eye care across the island through two mobile buses.
  • Training Facility Centre: Offers lecture halls, accommodation, and catering for workshops and training.
  • Ayurvedic Clinic: Provides free herbal medicines weekly for elders.
  • Youth Education Programme (YEP): Educates schoolchildren on caring for parents and elders.
  • Day Care Centre (DCC): Offers meals, activities, and medical checkups for elders in Ratmalana.
  • Home Care Service: Trains and deploys home carers with NVQ certification to assist senior citizens.
  • Advocacy Service: Supports the Government in improving elder welfare and rights.

Fundraising Activities

HelpAge Sri Lanka conducts multiple projects to sustain its mission:

  • Greeting Cards Project (since 1986)
  • Tills Project (since 1990)
  • Direct Mail Campaigns (since 1991)
  • Special CSR Projects (since 2011)
  • Give the Gift of Sight initiative

Foreign Funded Programmes

Implements donor-funded projects for livelihood support, leadership training, and community development, improving the lives of senior citizens nationwide.

HelpAge Sri Lanka is proud of its four decades of service to the elderly and remains committed to continuing and expanding these invaluable efforts.

Dr. Harsha Bandara

Executive Director
